Ingredients

  • 500g skinless chicken thigh, sliced into strips
  • 250g dried wheat noodles
  • 2 eggs
  • 3 medium onions
  • 1 banana shallot
  • 2 spring onions
  • 4 garlic gloves
  • 1cm piece of ginger
  • 200ml coconut milk
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 2 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp chicken bouillon
  • 2 tbsp sweet paprika
  • 1 tbsp gram flour

To serve

  • 1 lime, quartered
  • Crispy fried rice noodles
  • Chili oil

Steps

  1. Begin heating 1 tbsp of the oil in a large saucepan then thinly slice the onions and add to the fry for 10 minutes until soft.
  2. Gather 1 tbsp of the softened onions from the pan and grind to a paste with the spring onions, ginger, and garlic.
  3. Combine gram flour with 100ml cold water and add to the onions, along with the fish sauce and bouillon. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Soft boil the eggs and meanwhile thinly slice the shallot and add to a bowl of cold water.
  5. Heat a frying pan then fry the paste for a couple of minutes before adding 1 tbsp of the paprka then the slices chicken thigh. Mix to ensure the chicken is coated then fry until browned.
  6. Add the coconut milk to the saucepan then stir in with the remaining 1 tbsp of paprika, once the chicken is ready add this also.
  7. Continue to simmer the for 10 minutes while you prepare the noodles.
  8. Divide the cooked noodles between 4 deep bowls then divide the soup evenly between them, covering the noodles.
  9. Peel and slice the eggs into 4 halves, adding one to each bowl
  10. Drain the soaking shallots and add a small handful on top of each bowl, leaving the rest so that people may add more.
  11. Top each bowl with a handful of crispy fried rice noodles and lastly add a quarter of lime to be squeezed over before eating.
